The method

Some games are too good to leave on the table.

A love letter to the physical game — not a copy of it. The tactile feel, the players across from you, the energy in the room, the physicality — a digital companion that simply tries to copy that loses all of it the moment it leaves the table. One Day Games doesn't recreate the physical game. We channel its energy and translate it for the screen, rules and DNA intact. Reinterpreting the game — never reinventing it.

01 / KEEP

Hold the rules and DNA

The rule set and the DNA are sacred. That's the part you don't touch.

02 / TRANSLATE

The feel for the screen

Let go of what a screen can't carry. Then craft the feeling native to tap and click — the tempo, the texture, the small frictions that made it good in the hand.

03 / EXECUTE

One hand, vision to ship

One person interprets the game, builds it, and produces it. No handoff, no telephone game between the idea and the code, so nothing gets lost on the way to a real, playable release.

Live / plays in the browser

Tic-Tac-Toe

People have played tic-tac-toe for hundreds of years — Xs and Os scribbled on paper, in the sand, on foggy windows. Everywhere. I wanted to see it realized in a world of my own, on a screen, built in under a day. Original music, a friendly CPU, shipped and live — on the One Day Games site, mirrored on itch.io.

What it proves: One Day Games takes an idea all the way — finished, polished, and live in public. Not a prototype, not a pitch. And it can evolve a live game when there's a great idea to share: real updates — visuals, gameplay, mobile layout, review and readiness checks, release comms — moving through the pipeline cleanly. Grown because it's worth growing, not because a calendar says so.

The studio

A studio with a hand of its own.

One Day Games is the interpretive hand between a physical game and its digital life — a single point of view that owns the translation from first read to live release. Small footprint, real craft and real infrastructure underneath.

  • →One point of contact. Creative direction and production in one person. Nothing gets lost between the brief and the build.
  • →An autonomous pipeline. Releases self-monitor and self-heal — they catch their own failures and recover without anyone stepping in, so the studio scales without scaling headcount.
  • →AAA process, indie speed. The same production rigor used on multi-hundred-person teams, applied to nimble builds.
  • →One game at a time. No assembly line, no slate of parallel projects — each game gets a single maker's full attention, start to finish. Old-world focus, modern tools: AI handles the scale so the craft stays personal.
